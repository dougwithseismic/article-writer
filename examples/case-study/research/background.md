# Research: Why the World Didn't Need Another Issue Tracker

## Jira's Market Dominance (2019-2020)
- Jira led project management software with 36.57% market share in 2020
- Microsoft Project at 19.78%, Smartsheet at 5.17%
- In Germany, Jira had ~46.3% market share
- Market was expected to grow at CAGR of 10.67% from 2020-2025

## Competitive Landscape
- Microsoft Project: 18.66% market share
- Smartsheet: 9.26%
- Asana, Monday.com gaining traction in collaboration
- Notion Labs and Airtable attracting tech-savvy users
- GitHub Issues was free and integrated with developer workflows

## Jira Pain Points
- Load times measured in "coffee breaks"
- Hundreds of options creating "illusion of control" while slowing devs down
- Focused on satisfying managers and process consultants, not makers
- Adding required fields made the tool more burdensome with each interaction
- Overwhelming array of features led teams to spend more time managing workflows than developing
- Interface described as "overwhelmingly cluttered"

## Funding Timeline
- $4.2M Seed (Nov 2019) - led by Sequoia (Stephanie Zhan), with Index Ventures
- $13M Series A (2020) - Sequoia
- $35M Series B (Sept 2023) - led by Accel (Miles Clements), with Sequoia, 01Advisors
- $82M Series C (June 2025) - led by Accel, with 01A, Sequoia, Seven Seven Six, Designer Fund
- Total raised: $134.2M

## Failed Challengers
- Multiple issue tracker startups tried and failed before Linear
- The graveyard includes various tools that couldn't overcome switching costs and Jira's entrenched position
- GitHub Issues was free but limited in scope
